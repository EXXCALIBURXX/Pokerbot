Methodology & Context

Equity Source

Equity figures are approximate HU (heads up) equity vs a random opponent hand, computed across all runouts from the flop. Real equity shifts substantially based on board texture, villain's range, and position. These are ballpark figures for intuition-building.

Frequency Notes

Frequency = rough % of flop situations where you find yourself in this spot given your 2 hole cards. Premium made hands are rare (<1%). Top pair variants and draws are common (3–8%). Trash/air is the single most common category (~30%+ combined).

HU vs Full Ring

Heads up, ranges are much wider. Top pair weak kicker is genuinely strong HU. Ace high has showdown value. Underpairs are more playable. These equity numbers reflect HU specifically — in 6-max or full ring, downgrade weak made hands by 1 tier.

Board Texture Modifier

Dry boards (A 7 2 rainbow) inflate equity of made hands. Wet boards (J T 9 two-tone) deflate it. A set on a dry board has ~95% equity. That same set on a straight-flush board might be ~78%. Always adjust.

Combo Draws Are Underrated

Flush draw + OESD, pair + flush draw, two overcards + OESD — these combo draws often exceed 50% equity vs top pair. They are played as semi-bluffs but are mathematically favorites. Don't play them weakly.

The Real Game

Postflop isn't just "what do I have." It's range advantage + position + board texture. A player who c-bets every board and never folds is exploitable — recognize when the board favors your range, not just your specific hand.
